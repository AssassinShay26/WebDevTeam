How We Started

It all started in 2023, in a cramped garage on the outskirts of Columbus. I'm Beatrice, the lead vocalist of Stage Fright, and back then, none of us thought we'd make it past our first gig. Johnny, our bassist, was the one who convinced me to give rock a try. He'd been noodling around with his brother's old guitar when we met at a local open mic. His calm, steady vibe was the glue that held us together, even when everything else felt like chaos. Ronnie, on the other hand, was pure fire. He was already a local legend on the drums before we roped him in. You couldn't miss him—always tapping on tables or turning any surface into a makeshift drum kit. He had this energy that felt unstoppable, and when he joined us, it felt like the missing puzzle piece clicked into place.
The name "Stage Fright" came from a joke—we were all terrible at performing in front of people at first. Our first show was in a dingy bar with a broken PA system, but something clicked that night. Despite the shaky start, we saw people vibing to our music, and for the first time, it felt like we belonged somewhere. We weren't just a band; we became a family. Through late-night jam sessions, bad coffee, and worse gigs, we found our sound. It wasn't perfect, but it was raw and real—just like us. And that's all we ever wanted to be.
